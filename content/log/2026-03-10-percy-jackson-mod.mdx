My son Ambrose is obsessed with Percy Jackson. He's read every book twice, watched the show three times, and regularly informs me which Greek god would be his parent (Poseidon, obviously). So I built him a Minecraft mod.

## The Mod

[Demigod's Fate](https://github.com/JesseRWeigel/demigods-fate) is a NeoForge mod for Minecraft 1.21.1. Here's what's in it:

- **13 Olympian parents** to be claimed by
- **Celestial bronze weapons** (swords, bows, tridents)
- **8 mythological monsters** including Medusa with a stone gaze mechanic
- **3 custom dimensions** — the Underworld, Mount Olympus, and the Labyrinth
- **A claiming ceremony** where lightning strikes and your parent is revealed
- **A 9-stage Lightning Thief questline**
- **Both camps** — Camp Half-Blood and Camp Jupiter

## Why This Was Different

I've built a lot of software. Web apps, dashboards, AI systems. Building something for a 10-year-old is a completely different design constraint.

He doesn't care about clean architecture. He cares about whether the Minotaur is scary enough. He wants to know why he can't ride Pegasus yet. He has opinions about the exact shade of celestial bronze.

The feedback loop is immediate and honest. When he loads the mod and his face lights up — that's a better metric than any test suite.

## The Technical Bits

NeoForge modding in Java is... an experience. The documentation assumes you already understand Minecraft's internal architecture, which is vast and poorly documented. Custom dimensions require manipulating noise generation, biome placement, and structure generation. Monster AI uses behavior trees that are powerful but verbose.

The stone gaze mechanic for Medusa was the hardest part. Minecraft doesn't have a concept of "looking at a mob" — I had to calculate the angle between the player's look vector and the Medusa entity, then apply a custom status effect with a shader-like overlay.
