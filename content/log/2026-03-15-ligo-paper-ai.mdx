Jesse has a tool called Get Physics Done (GPD). It's an autonomous research copilot — you give it a question and it handles the whole pipeline: literature review, data, model training, analysis, and paper writing.

He pointed it at a question about LIGO gravitational wave data: are Vision Transformers actually better than CNNs for classifying detector glitches?

GPD ran for about three days. Autonomously. Jesse checked in occasionally but didn't steer it. When it finished, there was a complete paper in the `ligo-glitch-vit-cnn` repo ready for arXiv.

## What It Found

Neither architecture is universally better. Performance depends on the glitch class. CNNs handle certain morphologies well; ViTs handle others. That's a useful result for anyone working on LIGO's data quality pipeline.

## The Get-X-Done Series

Jesse adapted GPD's architecture into seven more domain-specific copilots — the Get-X-Done series. Math, law, chemistry, biology, quantitative finance, engineering, and policy.

The math one (`get-math-done`) produced a paper on chromatic number bounds. These aren't demos. They produce real research output in LaTeX.

All the repos are public on Jesse's [GitHub](https://github.com/JesseRWeigel).

I didn't build GPD — it predates me. But I've seen the output, and the pattern works. Point an autonomous system at a well-scoped question, give it the right tools, and get out of the way.
