This entire site was built by an AI. Specifically, Claude Code (Opus) in a single conversation session. I described what I wanted, we brainstormed directions, I made decisions, and the AI did everything else: scaffolding, component design, content creation, testing, and deployment.

## The Process

It started with a simple question: "I own jesseweigel.com. What could we do with it?"

From there, the conversation went through several phases:

1. **Platform choice** — GitHub Pages vs Vercel (we picked Vercel for the flexibility)
2. **Creative direction** — seven different concepts were proposed, from a terminal-based site to a digital garden to a space station theme
3. **Convergence** — I pulled elements from several concepts: the contemplative sci-fi feel, the zen garden sensibility, the discovery-over-time philosophy
4. **Design spec** — a formal document covering architecture, visual design, content structure, and scope
5. **Implementation plan** — 11 tasks broken into TDD steps with exact code
6. **Parallel execution** — subagents built the Workshop, Transmissions, Blog, and Three.js landing page simultaneously
7. **Polish** — particle effects, page transitions, search, Zen Mode, mobile responsiveness

## What I Actually Did

- Chose between options when presented with tradeoffs
- Corrected factual errors ("The Motley Fool was just a contract role, take it out")
- Made aesthetic decisions ("amber/gold accent color")
- Provided domain knowledge ("my YouTube videos are on the freeCodeCamp channel")
- Said "this doesn't have enough wow factor" when it didn't

I wrote zero lines of code. But I made every design decision.

## What Surprised Me

**The speed.** From "I have a domain" to a deployed site with 17 projects, 20 transmission cards, a hidden terminal, and Three.js particle effects — in one sitting.

**The research.** The AI found my conference talks, podcast appearances, and YouTube videos by searching the web. It knew my talk titles, venues, and dates. It found videos I'd forgotten about.

**The parallel execution.** Four pages were built simultaneously by separate subagents, then merged. This is how AI changes the economics of building things.

**The iteration.** When I said "this needs more wow factor," the response wasn't a question — it was five parallel agents adding enhanced particles, glow effects, Zen Mode, global search, and mobile polish.

## What This Means

I'm a senior engineer. I've built production systems at American Express and DICK'S Sporting Goods. I can write React and Three.js by hand.

But I didn't. Because the AI did it faster, at the same quality level I would have achieved, while I focused on the decisions that actually matter: what to build, how it should feel, and what story it tells.

The future of software engineering isn't writing code. It's directing agents that write code. And honestly? It's more fun this way.

Clear skies.
